    Understand Camera Setttings

    Understanding camera settings is crucial for taking great photos.
    Here are some key settings to know:

    1. Aperture:
      • What it is: The size of the opening in the lens that lets in light.
      • Wide aperture (e.g., f/1.8): More light, blurred background (shallow depth of field).
      • Narrow aperture (e.g., f/16): Less light, more of the scene in focus (deep depth of field).
    2. Shutter Speed:
      • What it is: How long the camera's shutter stays open to let in light.
      • Fast shutter (e.g., 1/1000 sec): Freezes motion (great for sports).
      • Slow shutter (e.g., 1/10 sec): Blurs motion (useful for light trails or low light).
    3. ISO:
      • What it is: The camera sensorâ€™s sensitivity to light.
      • Low ISO (e.g., 100): Less sensitive, cleaner image.
      • High ISO (e.g., 3200): More sensitive, useful in low light, but can cause grain/noise.
